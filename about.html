About

Husband. Father.
Builder.

Before any title I've held or product I've shipped, those three words are the ones that matter most to me. Everything else is built on top of them.

Where I come from

I am married to Petra and a father to Mia and Bodie. They are my why — the reason I work hard, stay curious, and try to build things that matter.

I am a first-generation Nicaraguan refugee turned immigrant and American citizen. The son of Daisy, who cleaned houses to make ends meet, and Juan, who toiled late evenings at a slaughterhouse until his wrist gave out. A brother to Maritza, a newly minted RN. I didn't come from tech. I came from people who worked with their hands so I could work with my mind — and I carry that with me every single day.

That origin shapes how I work. I don't take shortcuts. I don't treat users as abstractions. And I never forget that on the other side of every screen is a real person trying to do something that matters to them.

What I do

I am, genuinely, a unicorn in the tech world. I am a designer, a front-end developer, and a product manager — all three, at the same time, for real. That combination is rare, and I've spent my career leaning into it rather than picking a lane.

I've spent over a decade in digital transformation and e-commerce — building the apps, platforms, and systems that millions of people interact with every day, mostly without ever knowing my name. That's the job. You succeed when the product gets out of the way and just works.

What drives me is solving complex experience problems with great design and real technology, and doing it alongside people I can learn from — and mentor in return. Some of the most meaningful work I've done hasn't shipped a feature. It's helped someone else find their footing as a product leader.
